Cybersecurity Basics

Protecting Digital Systems & Information

Cybersecurity involves protecting your systems, networks, and data from digital attacks. As threats grow more complex, understanding these basic concepts is essential for everyone — not just IT professionals.

1. What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity refers to the practices and technologies used to protect systems and data from unauthorized access, attacks, and damage. Learn more →

2. Common Threats

  • Phishing: Fake emails or links that trick users into giving away sensitive information.
  • Malware: Harmful software such as viruses, worms, and spyware.
  • Ransomware: Attackers lock your files and demand payment to restore them.
  • Social Engineering: Tricking people into revealing passwords or access details.

3. Tools & Defenses

  • Antivirus Software: Detects and removes malicious software.
  • Firewalls: Block unauthorized access to your network.
  • Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Adds an extra layer of security during logins.

4. Cybersecurity Best Practices

  • Use strong, unique passwords and a password manager.
  • Keep your software and systems up to date.
  • Be cautious of suspicious links or attachments.
  • Back up important data regularly.
  • Educate employees and users about common cyber risks.
